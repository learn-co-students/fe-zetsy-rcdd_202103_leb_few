About

Mary Wanesia is Jeweler, an Arizona native, and a member of the Navajo tribe. She specializes in creating beautiful contemporary takes on the traditionnal adomments worn by Navajo in ceremonies and in daily life.


Jewelery in the Americas has an ancient history. Beginning as far back as 8800 BCE, Palco-Indians in the American Southwest drilled and shaped multicolored stones and shells into beads and pendants. Olivella shell beads, dating from 6000 BCE, were found in Nevada; bone, antler and possibly marine shell beads from 7000 BCE were found in Russell Cave in Alabama; copper jewelery was traded from Lake Superior beginning in 3000 BCE; and stone beads were carved in Poverty Point in Louisiana in 1500 BCE.Learn more.


Mary's jewelery haas received recognition, and been featured in the National Museum of the American Indian, The New York Times Style Magazine, and Sedona Monthly. The collection has been sold across all United States and Europe and been carried by the retail stores of Bergdore Goodman.
